A good neck gaiter or balaclava is one of those pieces of gear you don’t realize you need until you’re shivering on a windy chairlift or huffing through a frozen morning run. It seals the gap between your jacket collar and your hat, protects your face and neck from biting wind, and can be pulled up over your nose or mouth when the temperature really drops. The right one depends on how you’ll use it: synthetic fleece dries fast and breathes well for active use, merino wool offers natural odor resistance and softness for all-day wear, and thicker insulation like fleece or Polartec works best for standing around in deep cold. When choosing, think about fit (some are close-fitting like a balaclava, others sit looser like a tube), whether you want a reversible or convertible design, and how much breathability matters for your activity.
